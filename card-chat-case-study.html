Reimagining the Credit Card Listing Page as an AI Chat Experience

From Proof of Concept to MVP Pilot

Led as primary product designer alongside Head of Product to reframe a static credit card listing into a guided AI-supported decision experience.

AI Experience Content Modelling Interaction Design MVP Pilot

"The experience is optimized for exposure,
while users are trying to make a confident decision."

Core Problem

Listing pages are built to maximize visibility and monetization, not to help people understand tradeoffs, refine intent, or trust what they choose.

Overview

From Listing Page to Decision Experience

User Reality

Users arrive with

  • Clear intent — "best cashback card?"
  • Limited financial knowledge

Forced to compare dozens of cards → leading to

  • Choice paralysis → drop-offs
  • Suboptimal decisions → low satisfaction, churn

Business Reality

Competing Priorities

Promote high-margin cards

Visibility influenced by promotions and featured listings

Complex product data difficult to simplify without losing accuracy

Maintain fairness and trust

Project Vision

Reframing the listing page as a decision system

I reframed the credit card listing page into an AI-assisted decision system that guides users toward relevant options based on their needs and intent — augmenting the PLP with intelligence rather than replacing it.

Improving decision clarity is not just UX — it directly drives higher application completion and better card-user fit, reducing churn and mis-selling risk.

Designing the AI Decision System

From Listing Page → Decision Engine

An AI mediation layer embedded within the Product Listing Page — shifting the experience from passive browsing to guided decision-making.

System Logic Flow

AI must not just recommend — it must explain.

Interaction Concepts

Translating System Thinking into Interface Design

Four design decisions that shift the paradigm

01 Contextual Listings

Product Listings — From Static to Contextual

The Product Listing Page evolves from a catalogue of cards into a set of interpreted recommendations.

  • "Best for..." labels tied to user intent
  • Key benefits surfaced contextually
  • Information layered: Summary → Expand → Details
→

The listing becomes an answer to user intent, not just an option.

02 Embedded AI

AI Search & Chat — The Decision Guide

AI is embedded as a continuous intent refinement layer, not a separate chatbot.

A. Direct Search

"Best travel card with low fees" → Immediate results

B. Guided Flow

AI asks clarifying questions. Helps users refine their needs.

→

Chat controls the experience — it doesn't replace it.

03 Focused Comparison

Comparison — Focused, Not Exhaustive

Comparison narrows into a smaller guided choice set instead of forcing users through data-heavy tables.

Before

Dense tables with too many attributes

After

Limit to 2–3 cards only

Highlight only decision-driving differences

AI-generated insights: "Higher cashback on groceries"

→

Comparison becomes decision-oriented, not data-heavy.

04 Post-Selection

End-to-End Journey — Beyond Listing

The experience extends into reinforcing decisions and clarifying value post-selection.

→

Builds long-term trust and engagement.

Why This Approach Works

Four strategic advantages

Balances Business & User Needs

Business goals stay visible, but the logic for recommendations becomes easier to trust and defend.

Reduces Cognitive Load

AI simplifies the decision landscape while preserving user agency over the final choice.

Makes a Familiar Surface Smarter

The concept upgrades an existing listing pattern instead of forcing users into a completely foreign flow.

Creates a Scalable Foundation

The same decision-system logic can extend to loans, insurance, and other comparison-heavy financial products.

Outcome

MVP Pilot Validated

  • AI improves exploration and helps users narrow the field faster

  • Users rely on AI for clarification and confirmation — not blind trust

  • Explainability is critical to adoption in financial decision journeys

Designing AI for financial decisions is not about replacing user choice — it's about building systems that help users make better choices with confidence.
